Climate Change Research - Abstract

Title: Effects of Climate Change on Farmers in Bankra, Rajasthan

Research Period: February 2019

Methodology: Qualitative Research with Field Observations

Research Objective

This study investigated the multifaceted impacts of climate change-induced drought and water scarcity on the livelihoods of farming communities in Bankra village, Rajasthan. The research aimed to understand the direct and indirect effects of changing climate patterns on agricultural practices and farmer welfare.

Methodology

The research employed qualitative methods including in-depth interviews with local farmers and extensive field observations. This approach allowed for a comprehensive understanding of both the immediate challenges and long-term adaptations farmers have made in response to climate change.

Key Findings

  • Insufficient Irrigation Infrastructure: Farmers lack adequate irrigation facilities to cope with increasingly irregular rainfall patterns.
  • Water Scarcity Issues: Poor dam water management and inadequate rerouting systems have exacerbated water availability problems.
  • Financial Strain: Climate-related crop failures and reduced yields have placed significant economic pressure on farming families.
  • Adaptive Strategies: Farmers have developed various coping mechanisms, though many require additional support and resources.

Recommendations

The research proposed strategic dam water rerouting as a primary solution, along with improved irrigation infrastructure and climate-resilient farming practices. These findings contributed to broader discussions on climate adaptation strategies for rural agricultural communities.
